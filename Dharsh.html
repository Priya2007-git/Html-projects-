Selflove

Self-love is embracing your worth without needing validation from others. It’s about being kind to yourself, even on the days you feel unworthy.
Interestingly, people who engage in self-love rituals tend to have a more accurate memory. When you're kinder to yourself, you reduce the inner critic that distorts how you remember events, especially negative ones. As a result, your recollection of the past becomes more balanced and less emotionally biased. This unknown effect of self-love can help people heal from trauma or past regret more effectively than just therapy alone.
Another little-known fact is that self-love can subconsciously influence your voice tone and body language. Studies suggest that people with higher self-worth speak more slowly and clearly, with more controlled facial expressions—even when they’re unaware of it. This non-verbal confidence often leads others to perceive them as more trustworthy and competent. In other words, self-love silently improves how others respond to you, even in professional settings.
